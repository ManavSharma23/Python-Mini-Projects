The last train left the station just as the sky turned the color of burnt amber,
and for a moment the platform felt suspended outside of time. A stray newspaper skidded
along the concrete, chased by a restless wind that carried the faint smell of rain.
People who had been strangers minutes ago exchanged quiet glances, bound briefly by the
shared inconvenience of delay. Somewhere in the distance, thunder rolled like a slow drumbeat,
promising a storm that would wash the dust off the city and, perhaps, the worries off
those willing to stand in it
